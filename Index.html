Summary Revised

On May 11, 2026, National Communications Authority (ANACOM) initiated a public consultation regarding its proposed organizational guidelines for the implementation of Artificial Intelligence (AI) literacy. The guideline targets both AI providers and deployers who are responsible for ensuring the proper use and understanding of AI systems.

Comments may be sent by June 1, 2026, in writing and in Portuguese, by email to literaciaIA@anacom.pt. Organizations are encouraged to provide detailed feedback on the implementation framework, particularly on the risk and criticality assessment methodology.

Announcements
https://www.anacom.pt/render.jsp?contentId=1839581 https://www.anacom.pt/render.jsp?contentId=1839541 https://www.anacom.pt/render.jsp?contentId=1839530
Summary Details Revised

The guideline establishes a structured framework to help organizations comply with Article 4 of the EU AI Regulation. The AI Literacy Program steps are as follows:

  • Internal decision and enforcement order: Management formally approves the program and assigns responsibility.
  • Inventory of AI systems: List all AI tools used, including third-party and "shadow AI."
  • Internal risk and criticality ratings: Assess each system's risk based on impact, data use and autonomy.
  • Mapping of Profiles and Responsibilities: Define who uses, manages, supervises, and decides on AI outputs.
  • Defining objectives and minimum literacy levels: Set required AI knowledge levels per role and risk.
  • Internal rules of use and limits: Establish clear rules on allowed, restricted, and prohibited AI use.
  • Training plan: Provide ongoing, role-based training on AI use, risks, and validation.
  • Hiring and management of third parties: Ensure vendors and partners comply with AI rules.
  • Incident management: Define processes for reporting, logging, and resolving AI-related incidents.
  • Continuous monitoring and improvement: Regularly audit the program and update it based on new risks.
Affected Product Details
The draft guidelines apply to all public and private organizations that develop, supply or use AI systems, covering both providers and deployers under Article 4 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689.
